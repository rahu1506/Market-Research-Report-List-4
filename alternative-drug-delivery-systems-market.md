<p><p>Alternative drug delivery systems have different types such as nasal, transdermal, transmucosal, and others. Nasal drug delivery involves administering drugs through the nose, allowing for quick absorption into the bloodstream. Transdermal delivery involves applying drugs to the skin for slow and steady absorption. Transmucosal delivery involves administering drugs through mucous membranes, such as the mouth or rectum. Other types of alternative drug delivery systems include inhalation and implantable devices. These systems provide alternative routes for drug administration, offering benefits such as improved bioavailability and patient compliance.</p></p>
