<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Expansion Bolts Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Stud Anchors</li><li>Sleeved Anchors</li><li>Wedge Anchors</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Expansion bolts are commonly categorized into four main types:</p><p>1. Stud anchors: These bolts consist of a threaded stud that is inserted into a pre-drilled hole and secured with a nut. They are commonly used for heavy-duty applications that require high load-bearing capacity.</p><p>2. Sleeved anchors: These bolts feature a sleeve that expands when a screw or bolt is tightened, creating a secure anchor point. They are often used in medium to heavy-duty applications where a strong and reliable connection is needed.</p><p>3. Wedge anchors: These bolts have a wedge-shaped end that expands when tightened, providing a strong grip in concrete, brick, or stone. They are commonly used in construction and structural applications.</p><p>4. Other market: This category includes various types of expansion bolts that do not fall into the above-mentioned categories, such as drop-in anchors, flush anchors, and strike anchors. These bolts cater to specific needs and requirements based on the application and material being anchored to.</p></p>
