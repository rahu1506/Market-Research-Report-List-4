<p><p>Neurosurgery Electrosurgical Units (ESUs) are used in neurosurgical procedures to cut, coagulate, or seal tissues using high-frequency electrical current. There are three main types of ESUs: Monopole ESUs, which have one active electrode, Bipolar ESUs, which have two electrodes for precise coagulation, and Vessel Sealing Units, which use advanced technology to seal blood vessels during surgery. Each type has its own advantages and applications in neurosurgery, allowing surgeons to perform procedures safely and effectively.</p></p>
