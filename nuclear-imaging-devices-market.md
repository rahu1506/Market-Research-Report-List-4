<p><p>Nuclear imaging devices are used in the medical field for diagnostic imaging. Two common types are PET (Positron Emission Tomography) and SPECT (Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography). PET scans use radioactive tracers to detect cellular activity and metabolic changes in the body, making them useful for detecting cancer and brain disorders. On the other hand, SPECT scans use a gamma camera to create detailed images of organs and tissues, aiding in the diagnosis of conditions such as heart disease and neurological disorders.</p></p>
