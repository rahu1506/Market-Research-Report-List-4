<p><p>Vital signs monitoring systems are categorized based on the level of care they provide. High-acuity monitors are used in critical care settings to continuously monitor a patient's vital signs and provide real-time data on their condition. Mid-acuity monitors are typically used in step-down units or general care settings to monitor patients who are stable but still require close observation. Low-acuity monitors are used in outpatient or home settings to monitor chronic conditions or post-operative recovery. Each type of monitor caters to specific healthcare needs and settings.</p></p>
