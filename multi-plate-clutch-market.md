<p><p>A multi-plate clutch is a type of clutch system that consists of multiple friction plates sandwiched between alternating driven and driving plates. <p><p>The multi-plate clutch market can be categorized into different types based on their operating mechanism. Hydraulic clutches use fluid pressure to engage and disengage the plates. Spring-loaded clutches have a set of springs that apply pressure to the plates. Diaphragm type clutches use a diaphragm spring to engage the plates. There are also other variations of multi-plate clutches available in the market, each with their unique design and functionality. These variations cater to different needs and preferences of consumers in the automotive industry.</p></p>
