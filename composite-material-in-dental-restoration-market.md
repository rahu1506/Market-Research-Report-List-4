<p><p>Composite materials in dental restoration are commonly used for filling cavities and repairing damaged teeth. Two main types of composite materials are resin-based composites and poly-acid modified composites. Resin-based composites are made of a mixture of resin and filler particles, providing good aesthetics and strength. Poly-acid modified composites contain additional ingredients that help release fluoride ions, aiding in the prevention of further decay. Both types of composites are popular choices for dental restorations due to their durability, aesthetics, and biocompatibility.</p></p>
